I am interested in the theory of matrix polynomials and numerical solutions of the polynomial eigenvalue problem, which includes, as special cases, computing the roots of a polynomial and the eigenvalues of a matrix. Recently, I have applied my knowledge in numerical linear algebra and matrix polynomials to the study of the rankability of data and inhomogeneous Markov chains, respectively. My teaching experience spans a variety of academic institutions and mathematics courses, and a diverse student body.

About

I am a visiting assistant professor of Mathematics and Computer Science in the Department of Mathematics at Davidson College.

I am a passionate mathematician, educator, and programmer whose interests include linear algebra, numerical analysis, matrix polynomials, ranking, and nonnegative matrix theory. Current projects include the rankability of a dataset, Householder sets for matrix polynomials, and a compensated Horner method for accurate root solvers.
